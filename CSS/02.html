MAMAMOO

Debut

Mamamoo (Korean: 마마무, Japanese: ママム, commonly stylized in all caps as MAMAMOO) is a South Korean girl group formed and managed by Rainbow Bridge World. The group is composed of four members: Solar, Moonbyul, Wheein, and Hwasa. They officially debuted on June 18, 2014 with their first extended play (EP) Hello, which featured the lead s ingle "Mr. Ambiguous". Their debut was considered by critics as one of the best K-p op debuts of 2014.

Skills

Known for their strong live vocals, harmonies and powerful stage presence, Mamamoo is recognized as one of the premier girl groups in K-pop.[4][5] They are highly regarded in the industry for their talent, and ability to cross multiple genres with their music; from retro, jazz, R&B concepts in their early years[6] to more contemporary hip-hop,[7] as well as emotional ballads.

Achievements

Mamamoo rose to domestic fame in 2015 with their single "Um Oh Ah Yeah" which became a sleeper hit and peaked at number three on South Korea's Gaon Digital Chart. Combined with their many appearances and victories in the popular music competition program Immortal Songs, this launched them into mainstream recognition with the public.[10] Their following release of "You're the Best" (2016) from their debut studio album Melting, proved to be a commercial success and dominated at least eight major music sites upon release,[11] further cementing their popularity and growing momentum. In 2017, they made a splash on the international scene with their fifth EP Purple. Its lead single "Yes I Am" – a song about self-confidence and unashamedly being yourself, which the members took part in writing themselves – earned them their first number one on the Billboard World Albums chart.
